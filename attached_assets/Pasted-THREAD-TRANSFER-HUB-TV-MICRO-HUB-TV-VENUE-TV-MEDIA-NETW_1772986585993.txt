THREAD TRANSFER — HUB TV / MICRO HUB TV / VENUE TV MEDIA NETWORK
Scope: Add broadcast/media layer to the Hub ecosystem using YouTube streaming + hub-controlled discovery and monetization.

⸻

1. CORE CONCEPT

The platform is evolving into a local media network organized by micro hubs.

It is NOT a news organization and not a traditional media outlet.

The focus is:

• hyper-local storytelling
• businesses
• events
• experts
• venues
• community activity

Content is organized by place (micro hubs) rather than broad editorial categories.

There are 75 micro hubs that represent geographic neighborhoods and communities.

⸻

2. MEDIA NETWORK STRUCTURE

The system will operate three connected broadcast layers.
	1.	Hub TV (City Network)
	2.	Micro Hub TV (Neighborhood Channels)
	3.	Venue TV (Location Channels)

All three layers pull from the same content system but filter it differently.

⸻

3. HUB TV — CITY NETWORK

Hub TV is the main city-wide broadcast channel.

Primary characteristics:

• streaming channel powered by YouTube Live
• embedded on the hub platform
• accessible through the hub website and micro hubs
• programming rotates content from all micro hubs

Purpose:

• city-level media channel
• distribution of local stories
• platform for expert shows
• live broadcast events
• additional advertising inventory

Revenue sources:

• Hub TV ad placements
• show sponsors
• segment sponsors
• expert show participation
• social selling broadcasts

Content examples:

• business spotlight segments
• event highlights
• community interviews
• expert segments
• micro hub updates
• venue highlights

⸻

4. MICRO HUB TV — NEIGHBORHOOD CHANNELS

Each micro hub will have its own localized video stream.

Example:

South End Hub TV
Matthews Hub TV
Ballantyne Hub TV

These channels only display content tied to that specific micro hub.

Content sources:

• local businesses
• local events
• local venues
• local experts
• local stories

Purpose:

• create hyper-local identity
• strengthen neighborhood engagement
• support local advertising opportunities

Revenue sources:

• micro hub sponsors
• local ad placements
• featured business segments
• local expert shows

Micro Hub TV appears directly on the micro hub page inside the platform.

⸻

5. VENUE TV — LOCATION CHANNELS

Venue TVs are already part of the system and operate inside physical locations.

Examples:

bars
restaurants
gyms
waiting rooms
salons
entertainment venues

Venue screens display:

• venue announcements
• venue promotions
• local content from the micro hub
• hub programming segments
• advertising

Venues also include physical engagement through:

• QR table tents
• bathroom stall hangers
• entry decals
• counter cards

These connect visitors to the hub platform.

Revenue sources:

• venue screen advertising
• sponsor placements
• social selling events
• venue promotions

⸻

6. CONTENT DISTRIBUTION MODEL

Content should be reusable across the entire network.

Example:

A restaurant interview in South End.

It can appear in:

South End Micro Hub TV
Hub TV city stream
Pulse feed
restaurant profile page
nearby venue screens
YouTube replay

This allows one piece of content to power multiple channels.

⸻

7. CONTENT TYPES

The network will support several types of programming.

Examples:

business spotlight segments
community interviews
event coverage
venue highlights
expert shows
social selling segments

Experts may host short shows similar to radio programs that buy airtime.

Example expert segments:

real estate update
health tips
small business strategy
local restaurant highlights

These segments may appear on:

Hub TV
Micro Hub TV
podcasts
YouTube replays

⸻

8. PODCAST EXTENSION

Every video segment can also become a podcast episode.

Example workflow:

record video interview
→ broadcast on Hub TV
→ publish replay on YouTube
→ convert audio to podcast
→ post clips in Pulse feed

This creates a multimedia ecosystem using the same content source.

⸻

9. CONTENT ORGANIZATION

Content is tagged with the following identifiers:

cityId
microHubId
venueId (optional)
contentType
categoryTags (optional)

These tags determine where content appears.

Example logic:

If microHubId = SouthEnd

The content appears in:

South End Micro Hub TV
Hub TV city broadcast
South End Pulse feed
South End venue screens

⸻

10. PROGRAMMING LOOP

Channels will run a continuous rotation.

Typical programming order:

story segment
event highlight
business spotlight
venue segment
expert show
advertising block

Then repeat.

This is rotation-based programming, not algorithmic ranking.

⸻

11. DISTRIBUTION CHANNELS

Content will be distributed across several surfaces:

Hub TV stream
Micro Hub TV streams
Venue screens
Pulse feed
business pages
micro hub pages
YouTube replay library
podcast feeds

This creates multiple exposure layers.

⸻

12. STRATEGIC POSITION

The hub becomes a community broadcast network.

Not a directory.
Not a traditional media outlet.

Instead:

a hyper-local multimedia network organized around neighborhoods.

Components include:

Pulse feed (social layer)
Hub TV (city broadcast layer)
Micro Hub TV (neighborhood channels)
Venue screens (physical distribution layer)
podcasts (audio distribution layer)
marketplace / social selling (commerce layer)

⸻

13. IMPLEMENTATION PRIORITIES

Initial build focus:
	1.	Hub TV streaming infrastructure using YouTube Live
	2.	Micro Hub TV pages embedded in each micro hub
	3.	Content tagging for cityId / microHubId / venueId
	4.	automated programming rotation
	5.	ability to embed YouTube streams inside hub pages

Venues already support screen distribution.

This step extends the media network into city and neighborhood broadcast channels.
