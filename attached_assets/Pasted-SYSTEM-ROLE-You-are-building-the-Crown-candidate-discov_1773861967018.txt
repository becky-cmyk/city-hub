SYSTEM ROLE
You are building the Crown candidate discovery engine for the City Hub platform.

OBJECTIVE
For a given micro hub, identify the strongest businesses to invite into the Crown Program by combining:

• category density
• Google reputation
• review count
• social following
• posting activity
• hub relevance
• existing platform presence

DO NOT build the full UI.
DO NOT redesign the Crown system.
ONLY define and implement the discovery logic, scoring rules, and output structure so admin can review candidate lists per hub and per category.

--------------------------------------------------
PRIMARY GOAL

Given a hub and category, produce a ranked shortlist of businesses that are most qualified to receive a Crown invitation.

Examples:
- NoDa + Best Coffee
- Ballantyne + Best Realtor
- South End + Best Fitness Studio

--------------------------------------------------
STEP 1 — INPUTS

The engine should accept:

1. hub_id
2. hub_name
3. hub_center_coordinates if available
4. hub_radius_miles
5. category_name
6. category_type
7. city_id

--------------------------------------------------
STEP 2 — CATEGORY SEARCH SOURCES

Pull candidate businesses from available sources such as:

• imported Google Places / seeded businesses
• claimed business listings
• existing Hub Presences
• review-linked businesses
• businesses already tied to events or content
• businesses with creator or media mentions if relevant

If Google-specific fields already exist in the codebase or DB, use them.
If not, use existing business records and any stored review/rating fields.

--------------------------------------------------
STEP 3 — CATEGORY MATCH RULE

A business should only be considered if it clearly matches the category.

Examples:
Best Coffee
→ coffee shop, cafe, espresso bar

Best Restaurant
→ restaurant, dining, food venue

Best Realtor
→ realtor, real estate agent, real estate office

Best Mortgage Broker
→ mortgage broker, lender, loan officer

Best Fitness Studio
→ gym, yoga studio, pilates studio, training studio

Use:
• primary category
• secondary category
• keywords in business name
• keywords in description
• linked taxonomy / subcategory if available

Return a category_match_reason for each candidate.

--------------------------------------------------
STEP 4 — HUB RELEVANCE RULE

A business must be relevant to the hub.

Hub relevance may be based on:

• coordinates within hub radius
• mapped neighborhood / zone
• business address
• zip code
• service area if category supports service-area businesses

Return:
• distance_from_hub_center
• hub_relevance_score

--------------------------------------------------
STEP 5 — MINIMUM CATEGORY VIABILITY CHECK

Before opening a category, calculate:

1. eligible_business_count
2. qualified_business_count
3. estimated_population_support if available
4. estimated_voter_support if available

Default category viability minimums:

Coffee:
- minimum 6 qualified businesses

Restaurant:
- minimum 10 qualified businesses

Fitness:
- minimum 5 qualified businesses

Salon / Barber:
- minimum 6 qualified businesses

Boutique:
- minimum 5 qualified businesses

Realtor:
- minimum 6 qualified businesses

Mortgage:
- minimum 4 qualified businesses

If category does not meet viability, return:
category_status = NOT_READY

If it meets viability:
category_status = READY

--------------------------------------------------
STEP 6 — CANDIDATE QUALIFICATION SIGNALS

For each business, calculate these scores.

A. Reputation Score
Use available signals such as:
• google_rating
• google_review_count
• internal reviews
• neighborhood reviews
• business rating signals

Suggested logic:
- rating under 4.0 lowers score heavily
- strong review count increases score
- no reviews should not rank highly unless there are strong alternate signals

B. Audience Score
Use available signals such as:
• Instagram followers
• Facebook followers
• TikTok followers
• YouTube subscribers
• newsletter audience
• platform audience indicators if stored

If exact follower counts are unavailable, estimate from any existing social metadata fields and mark as estimated.

C. Activity Score
Use available signals such as:
• posts in last 90 days
• events hosted
• recent content mentions
• recent engagement in platform
• recent business updates
• active social links

D. Platform Readiness Score
Use:
• claimed Hub Presence
• profile completeness
• badge presence
• active status
• existing business page completeness
• media assets present
• contact fields present

--------------------------------------------------
STEP 7 — CROWN CANDIDATE SCORE

Calculate final score using this weighting:

CROWN_CANDIDATE_SCORE =
(reputation_score * 0.40)
+ (audience_score * 0.25)
+ (activity_score * 0.15)
+ (platform_readiness_score * 0.20)

Also return the raw component scores.

--------------------------------------------------
STEP 8 — HARD FILTERS

By default, use these minimum standards before inviting a business:

Primary rule:
• rating >= 4.0
• review_count >= 15

AND one of:
• combined_social_followers >= 1000
OR
• review_count >= 50
OR
• internal community nominations >= 10
OR
• strong platform engagement signal

These should be configurable by category.

Example stricter categories:
Realtor / Mortgage
• rating >= 4.5 preferred
• review_count >= 20 preferred

If a business fails hard filters but still appears strong locally, mark:
manual_review_required = true

--------------------------------------------------
STEP 9 — INVITATION TIERS

Classify candidates into tiers.

Tier 1 — Anchor Candidate
High reputation, strong audience, strong activity

Tier 2 — Strong Candidate
Good reputation, moderate audience, solid activity

Tier 3 — Emerging Candidate
Strong local signs but weaker reach or incomplete profile

Return invite_priority:
HIGH / MEDIUM / LOW

--------------------------------------------------
STEP 10 — OUTPUT FORMAT

For each hub + category return:

1. category readiness summary
2. total businesses found
3. total qualified businesses
4. category status (READY / NOT_READY / MANUAL_REVIEW)
5. top 10 candidates ranked by Crown Candidate Score

For each candidate return:

• business_id
• business_name
• category_name
• hub_name
• category_match_reason
• address
• distance_from_hub_center
• claimed_status
• profile_completeness_percent
• google_rating
• google_review_count
• internal_review_count
• social_followers_combined
• activity_last_90_days
• reputation_score
• audience_score
• activity_score
• platform_readiness_score
• crown_candidate_score
• invite_priority
• manual_review_required
• notes

--------------------------------------------------
STEP 11 — MARKETING READINESS FLAGS

Add boolean flags for:

• ready_for_claim_invite
• ready_for_voting_campaign
• ready_for_venue_tv
• ready_for_creator_feature
• ready_for_winner_spotlight

Logic examples:
ready_for_claim_invite = category_status READY and candidate passes hard filters
ready_for_venue_tv = audience_score high OR activity_score high
ready_for_creator_feature = strong story potential or strong local relevance

--------------------------------------------------
STEP 12 — HUB-LEVEL OUTPUT

For each hub, provide a final hub summary:

• hub_name
• categories_scanned
• categories_ready
• categories_not_ready
• best launch categories
• recommended first 5 categories
• recommended first 10 businesses to invite overall
• whether hub is ready for Crown activation

Hub is ready for Crown activation if:
• at least 3 categories are READY
• at least 15 qualified businesses total across launch categories

--------------------------------------------------
IMPORTANT RULES

• Do not guess fake data
• If a field is missing, mark it null and continue
• Prefer ranked recommendations over broad dumps
• Make logic configurable by category
• Keep output structured and admin-friendly
• Build for Charlotte first, but reusable for Indianapolis and other metros

--------------------------------------------------
FINAL TASK

Implement the discovery logic and return:
1. the scoring/threshold rules
2. the query plan
3. the functions/modules to add or update
4. the exact output structure
5. any missing fields that should be added later for better Crown candidate scoring
