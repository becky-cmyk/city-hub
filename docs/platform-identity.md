# Platform Identity — Positioning Mental Model

## Core Identity

City Hub is what the local section of a newspaper used to be — rebuilt as a living platform for each community.

## Historic Reference

Traditional metro newspapers often included smaller local sections or inserts for specific areas, towns, or districts. Those sections highlighted local stories, local businesses, local events, community notices, and neighborhood-level happenings. That is the closest familiar model for understanding City Hub.

## The Difference

City Hub does not treat that local section as a small insert inside a larger paper.
City Hub turns that local section into the core platform for the area itself.

Each micro hub / district hub should be understood as:
- A living local section
- Always updating instead of static
- Searchable instead of buried
- Interactive instead of one-way
- Multi-channel instead of print-only

## What City Hub Is NOT (In Isolation)

City Hub is not just:
- A directory
- A feed
- A marketplace
- An event board
- A jobs board
- A media brand

It is the modern local section of a newspaper expanded into a full community distribution system.

## Correct Framing

- Community newspaper at the core
- Local section / district insert as the closest historic analogy
- Rebuilt as a modern, always-on platform
- Distributed across print, digital, physical placements, venue TV, and radio
- Designed so stories, businesses, events, jobs, creators, marketplace activity, and local offers all live inside the same place-based ecosystem

## Engine-to-Newspaper Mapping

Each engine in City Hub maps to what a local newspaper section used to contain:

| Engine | Newspaper Equivalent |
|--------|---------------------|
| **Hub Presence** | Business listing / yellow pages (identity layer) |
| **Pulse** | The living stream / heartbeat (what's happening now) |
| **Directory + Categories** | Yellow pages organized by place, not interest |
| **Charlotte Recommendations** | Neighbor word-of-mouth / trusted local tips |
| **Marketplace** | Classifieds section |
| **Events** | Community calendar |
| **Stories / Articles** | Local reporting and features |
| **Media Network** (radio, podcasts, music, venue TV) | Distribution across channels |
| **Jobs / Workforce** | Help wanted section |
| **Print + Digital + TV + Radio + Physical Placements** | Multi-channel distribution (not just digital) |

## Core Identity to Preserve

- **Hub Presence** = identity layer
- **Pulse** = living stream / heartbeat
- **Print + digital + TV + radio + physical placements** = distribution layer
- **City Hub** = modern local section / community newspaper system for each district or micro hub

## Explanation Lines

Use these when describing the platform:
- "City Hub is the local section of a newspaper turned into a full platform for each community."
- "We took what newspapers used to do in local inserts and rebuilt it as a living, modern hub."
- "This is not just digital publishing; it is community distribution across print, digital, TV, radio, and real-world placements."
- "Each micro hub functions like its own always-updating local section."

## Anti-Drift Guidance

Do not drift back into explaining City Hub as only a directory, only a media company, only a marketplace, or only a social feed. Those are components or surfaces inside the system, not the core identity.

## Short Internal Summary

City Hub is what a newspaper's local section used to do — but now it is the full platform, the distribution engine, and the living record of the community.
